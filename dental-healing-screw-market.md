<p><p>Dental healing screws are used in implant dentistry to secure artificial teeth to the jawbone. They come in two main types - Titanium and Zirconium. Titanium screws are the most commonly used due to their strength, biocompatibility, and ability to integrate with bone tissue. Zirconium screws, on the other hand, are known for their natural tooth-like appearance and biocompatibility. Both types of screws play a crucial role in providing stable and long-lasting dental restorations for patients.</p></p>
