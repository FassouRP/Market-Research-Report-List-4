<p><p>Dental X-ray machines come in two main types: Ordinary X-rays and Panoramic X-rays. Ordinary X-rays, also known as intraoral X-rays, are used to capture images of individual teeth and small sections of the mouth. Panoramic X-rays, on the other hand, provide a full view of the entire mouth and jaw in a single image. Panoramic X-rays are often used for a comprehensive assessment of oral health and for planning treatments such as orthodontics or oral surgery. Both types of X-ray machines play a crucial role in modern dental practices for diagnosing and treating various oral health conditions.</p></p>
