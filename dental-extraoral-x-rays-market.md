<p><p>Dental extraoral X-rays are divided into two main types: panoramic and cone beam computed tomography (CBCT). Panoramic X-rays provide a broad view of the entire mouth, jaw, and teeth in a single image, making it useful for assessing overall dental health and detecting issues such as impacted teeth or jaw fractures. On the other hand, CBCT X-rays offer a more detailed 3D view of specific areas, making them ideal for planning complex dental procedures like implants or oral surgeries. Both types of X-rays play a crucial role in dental diagnostics and treatment planning.</p></p>
