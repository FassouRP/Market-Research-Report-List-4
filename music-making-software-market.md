<p><p>Music-making software can be categorized into three main types: editing, mixing, and recording. Editing software allows musicians to manipulate and refine their audio recordings by cutting, copying, and pasting different sections of a track. Mixing software enables users to adjust the levels, effects, and panning of individual tracks to create a cohesive sound. Recording software allows musicians to capture and save their performances digitally, often with features such as multitrack recording and virtual instruments. Each type of software serves a specific function in the music production process.</p></p>
