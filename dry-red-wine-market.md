<p><p>Dry red wine is categorized into two main market types: Still Wines and Sparkling Wines. Still Wines retain their natural carbon dioxide content and do not undergo a second fermentation process, resulting in a non-effervescent drink. Sparkling Wines, on the other hand, undergo a secondary fermentation which creates carbonation within the bottle. Both types offer a variety of options for consumers, with Still Wines providing a more traditional and rich taste, while Sparkling Wines offer a more lively and bubbly drinking experience.</p></p>
