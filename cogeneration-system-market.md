<p><p>Cogeneration systems are classified into five main types based on the technology used: Steam Turbine, Gas Turbine, Fuel Cell, Reciprocating Engine, and Others. Steam Turbine systems use steam to generate electricity and heat simultaneously. Gas Turbine systems use gas combustion to generate electricity and heat. Fuel Cell systems produce electricity through a chemical reaction. Reciprocating Engine systems use internal combustion engines to generate electricity and heat. Other types of cogeneration systems include microturbines, Stirling engines, and Organic Rankine Cycle systems.</p></p>
