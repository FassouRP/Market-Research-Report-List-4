<p><p>Buccal drug delivery systems consist of various types such as sublingual films, tablets, and sprays. Sublingual films are thin films placed under the tongue for quick absorption, while tablets are placed between the cheek and gum to dissolve slowly. Sprays are used to deliver medication to the inner lining of the cheek. These systems provide a convenient and effective way to administer drugs, allowing for rapid absorption and bypassing the digestive system. They are often used for drugs that have poor bioavailability or stability in the gastrointestinal tract.</p></p>
