<p><p>Plug-in relays are commonly classified into two main types: DC relays and AC relays. DC relays are designed to work with direct current electrical systems, while AC relays are specifically made for alternating current applications. Both types of relays come in various configurations to suit different voltage and current requirements. DC relays are typically used in automotive applications and electronic devices, while AC relays are commonly found in household appliances and industrial automation systems. Both types play a crucial role in controlling electrical circuits efficiently and safely.</p></p>
